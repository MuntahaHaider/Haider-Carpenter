Partex wood

Partex Wood, also known as Particleboard, is an engineered wood product made from small wood particles, such as sawdust, shavings, and chips, pressed together with a binding agent. It's a cost-effective and eco-friendly alternative to solid wood. Partex Wood is denser and heavier than Chipboard Wood, making it suitable for load-bearing applications. Its advantages include:

  • Affordability
  • Durability
  • Resistance to warping and shrinking
  • Easy to work with





Chipboard Wood

Chipboard Wood, also known as Low-Density Fiberboard (LDF), is another engineered wood product made from wood chips and shavings pressed together with a binding agent. It's lighter and less dense than Partex Wood, making it ideal for non-load-bearing applications. Chipboard Wood is:

  • Budget-friendly
  • Easy to cut and shape
  • Suitable for decorative purposes
  • Less durable than Partex Wood





Particle Wood

Particle Wood, also known as Medium-Density Fiberboard (MDF), is a type of engineered wood made from wood fibers pressed together with a binding agent. It's denser than Chipboard Wood but less dense than Partex Wood. Particle Wood is:

  • Smooth and dense
  • Ideal for painting and finishing
  • Resistant to warping and shrinking
  • Suitable for furniture and cabinetry





summary

In summary, Partex Wood is a denser and heavier option suitable for load-bearing applications, while Chipboard Wood is a lighter and more budget-friendly option ideal for decorative purposes. Particle Wood offers a balance between density and affordability, making it suitable for furniture and cabinetry.
